## SERP / skyscraper references
Search results for `AI web chat lead capture website visitors qualified conversations CRM small business` surfaced mostly vendor and list-style pages. The most relevant references:

1. Salesforce — Best AI sales chatbot
   - URL: https://www.salesforce.com/sales/ai/best-ai-sales-chatbot/
   - Summary: Frames sales chatbots as tools that engage web visitors, qualify inbound leads, answer questions, and automate steps in the sales process.
   - Gap: Strong tool framing, but less operator guidance on handoff rules and what not to automate.

2. HubSpot — Live chat product page
   - URL: https://www.hubspot.com/products/crm/live-chat
   - Summary: Connects website visitors to the right team, routes service inquiries, and passes leads to sales.
   - Gap: Useful category explanation, but product-led and generic. Does not tie chat into calls, SMS, and CRM handoff.

3. Zendesk — AI chatbots for sales
   - URL: https://www.zendesk.com/service/messaging/ai-chatbot-for-sales/
   - Summary: Discusses AI sales chatbots and real-time visitor connection.
   - Gap: Focused on support/sales software comparison, not the first-response layer for small businesses.

4. dotfun Iris
   - URL: https://www.dotfun.co/iris
   - Summary: Iris handles calls, SMS, web chat, lead capture, routing, logging, and notifications.

## Coverage gaps to hit
- Treat chat as a lead capture workflow, not a floating website widget.
- Explain when chat should collect info, when it should answer, and when it should hand off.
- Connect web chat to CRM logging, SMS follow-up, call routing, and notifications.
- Include compliance guidance for SMS/contact consent.
- Give a practical first-message and qualification structure.
- Avoid claiming AI replaces all human judgment.
