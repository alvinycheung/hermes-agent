# dotfun Website Expansion Walkthrough

## Confirmed architecture decisions

- dotfun is the parent brand.
- Iris is the software developed by dotfun.
- The three website wings are:
  - Iris Platform — software with feature pages.
  - dotfun AI Solutions — AI solution pages.
  - dotfun Growth Services — solution pages and service pages.

## 1. Parent brand

- What should visitors understand about dotfun within 5 seconds?
- How directly should the homepage route users into the three wings?
- What proof can dotfun use to support the parent-brand claim that it builds AI growth systems?
- Which tone is too playful, too corporate, or just right?

## 2. Iris Platform

- What features are real now vs roadmap?
- Which feature pages are launch-ready: AI CRM, AI receptionist, web chat, SMS, forms, calendar, dashboards, sequences, analytics?
- Which ICP should `/iris` prioritize now: SMB services, funded startups, or mid-market B2B?
- Should vertical pages remain under Iris for home services, legal, property management, etc.?

## 3. dotfun AI Solutions

- Which solution pages are real now?
- Which use cases deserve pages: AI agents, RAG/ETL, automation, systems integration, voice/SMS agents, dashboards, semantic search, app development?
- What proof/case studies can be shown?
- How should AI Solutions link to Iris Platform without confusing custom services with software features?

## 4. dotfun Growth Services

- Which current dotfun service pages should survive?
- Should Feel Good / Look Good / Run Good remain internally, publicly, or be retired?
- Which services need service pages vs solution pages?
- Which services are premium/retainer-only?

## 5. SEO/research

- SEMRush/GSC/GA4 availability?
- Priority competitors beyond the seed set?
- Priority markets and launch pages?
