# dotfun Workbook Quality Gates

## Gate 1 — Source traceability

Every claim has a source, provided context, research evidence, or explicit assumption tag.

## Gate 2 — Three-wing clarity

Every major URL/cluster has a wing owner: `parent-brand`, `iris-platform`, `dotfun-ai-solutions`, `dotfun-growth-services`, or `cross-wing`.

## Gate 3 — Page-type clarity

- Iris Platform recommendations distinguish platform pages, feature pages, use-case pages, industry pages, and comparison pages.
- dotfun AI Solutions recommendations distinguish overview pages, solution pages, workflow/use-case pages, and proof pages.
- dotfun Growth Services recommendations distinguish overview pages, solution pages, service pages, case studies, and supporting content.

## Gate 4 — Workbook completeness

Required tabs: brand/wing map, keyword domination plan, content architecture, URL structure, competitive gap execution, wing positioning matrix, internal linking matrix, schema coverage matrix, research log, QA report.

## Gate 5 — Depth

Initial targets: 45–90 keyword rows, 30–70 architecture rows, 15+ competitive gap rows, 40+ internal linking rules, schema for all major page templates.

## Gate 6 — Execution practicality

Separate launch vs phase-two work, redirect/canonical implications, dependencies, owner, priority, and confidence.

## Verdict

Use READY / NOT READY. Never mark ready with unresolved blockers.
